Kana

Kana are the syllabaries that form part of the japanese writing system, contrasted with the logographic chinese characters known in Japan as kanji.
Each kana character (syllabogram) corresponds to one sound in the japanese language, that's why the character system is named kana, literally "false name". Due to the limited number of phonemes in Japanese, as well as the relatively rigid syllable structure, the kana system is a very accurate representation of spoken Japanese.
There are 2 kanas you should know before doing anything else:

  • Hiragana (used for native japanese words)
  • Katakana (used mostly for foreign words)

Kanji

Kanji are the adopted logographic chinese characters that are used as one of the japanese writing systems. Logographic meaning that each kanji character means a specific thing, for example 犬 (dog). The exact number of kanji is not known, but at least 50,000 are known to exist, but don't worry, you "only" need to know 2,136 characters known has the " jōyō kanji". You can learn them from here:
